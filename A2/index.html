How inbound tourists travel to Malaysia?

Land travel is the primary mode of transportation for inbound tourists. The dominance of land travel suggests that a significant portion of Malaysia's tourists likely come from neighbouring countries, particularly Singapore and Thailand. This reliance on land travel could make Malaysia's tourism industry vulnerable to border closures or restrictions.

Users can select the data of desired year using the drop down menu.

Where did domestic tourists stay?

From 2015 to 2022, staying with relatives and friends was the top choice for domestic tourists in Malaysia. Rest houses rose in popularity, ranking fifth in 2019 and second in 2020 due to affordability and rural locations, but dropped to sixth in 2021 during the pandemic. They regained interest post-2021, suggesting a revival among travelers.

Users can click on the legend to highlight the desired accommodation type.

How currency affects Singapore's tourists?

Generally, as SGD to MYR currency began to rise,
the number of tourists also increased. In December,
there is typically a significant increase in tourist numbers
due to school holidays in Singapore. In 2018 and 2019,
the number of tourists decreased despite the rising SGD currency,
likely because other competing travel destinations, such as Thailand
and Indonesia, became popular among Singaporeans during that period.

Where did tourists spend their money?

Inbound and outbound tourists typically spend on country-specific tourism characteristics goods.
Specifically, inbound tourists tend to spend more on accommodation and transportation.
The expenditures sharply reduced due to the COVID-19 pandemic from 2020 to 2021.
