Annual Inbound Tourists to Malaysia

The chart below illustrates the inbound tourist flow for the selected year. Users can zoom in on the map using the slider to get a clearer view of the flow from countries neighboring Malaysia. Singapore consistently leads in tourist numbers, except in 2021 when Thailand surpassed it due to the closure of Singapore's borders during the COVID-19 pandemic.

How inbound tourists travel to Malaysia?

Land travel is the primary mode of transportation for inbound tourists. The dominance of land travel suggests that a significant portion of Malaysia's tourists likely come from neighbouring countries, particularly Singapore and Thailand. This reliance on land travel could make Malaysia's tourism industry vulnerable to border closures or restrictions. Users can select the data of desired year using the drop down menu.

Where did domestic tourists stay?

From 2015 to 2022, staying with relatives and friends was the top choice for domestic tourists in Malaysia. Rest houses rose in popularity, ranking fifth in 2019 and second in 2020 due to affordability and rural locations, but dropped to sixth in 2021 during the pandemic. They regained interest post-2021, suggesting a revival among travelers. Users can click on the legend to highlight the desired accommodation type.

How currency affects Singapore's tourists?

In the chart below, users can click and drag on the line chart to examine the correlation between the SGD currency and the number of Singaporean tourists visiting Malaysia. Generally, as the SGD currency began to rise, the number of tourists also increased. However, the data for 2020 and 2021 is less visible due to a significant decline in tourist numbers caused by the COVID-19 pandemic. Furthermore, in 2018 and 2019, the number of tourists decreased despite the rising SGD currency, likely because other competing travel destinations, such as Thailand and Indonesia, became popular among Singaporeans during that period.

Where did tourists spend their money?

Inbound tourists tend to spend on accommodations and transportation. The expenditures sharply reduced due to the COVID-19 pandemic from 2020 to 2021.
